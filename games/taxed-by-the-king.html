Taxed by the King rules

A card-based risk and reward game for exploring probability, dependent probability, addition, doubling, negative numbers, and strategic thinking.

  • The teacher creates a room and students join with a game code.
  • The teacher starts each round and turns over one card at a time from a shuffled 54-card deck.
  • Ace is worth $1. Number cards 2–10 are worth their face value.
  • Jack adds a $20 bonus to the current round total.
  • Queen doubles the current round total. If the total is negative, the Queen doubles the negative.
  • Joker takes $20 from the current round total. The teacher can choose whether Jokers are allowed to make the total negative.
  • King collects his tax. Students still standing get $0 for that round. Students who already banked are safe.
  • Students may bank at any time after a card has resolved. Once banked, they are safe until the next round.
  • If all students bank, the round ends. The teacher presses Start Next Round to continue.
  • The game ends immediately when the fourth King is revealed.
  • After each completed round, the highest scorer for that round chooses an unused rank from A–10 and gives it a new rule. These rules are cumulative.
