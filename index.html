About this site

This site hosts a growing collection of classroom-tested visual mathematics tools designed to support deep conceptual understanding in Years 7–10.

The tools are aligned to the ELPSA learning cycle (Experience, Language, Pictorial, Symbolic, Application), providing multiple entry points for students and supporting movement from concrete experiences to formal mathematical reasoning.

Design decisions are informed by Di Siemon’s Big Ideas in Mathematics, with an emphasis on structure, relationships, and multiplicative thinking.

  • Make mathematical structure visible
  • Support discussion, reasoning, and representation
  • Reduce cognitive load while maintaining conceptual demand
  • Be usable flexibly for whole-class teaching, small groups, or independent exploration
